# Owen's Steno Keyboard Mk. 1
The PCB, firmware, casing, as well as the keycaps are all made by myself, from scratch.  
The PCB schematics and design, CAD step files, and firmware are stored within their retrospective folders.

* On the bottom-most row, the keys on both sides that are furthest away from the center are the number keys.  
* The two double length keys in the middle (one on each side) is the '*' key.
* The other keys follow standard steno layout.

* The decision to have each switch have their own GPIO port is just for ease of assembly and that there was no reason not to do so.  

## Bill of Materials 
- 4 M3 x 12mm screw
- 4 M3 x 0.5mm nut
- 25 Mechanical switches (Cherry MX)
- 1 Raspberry Pi Pico
- 1 PCB
- 1 Micro USB cable
- 3 3D printed 2.5u Steno keycaps (OR 2u standard keycaps)
- 22 3D printed 1.5u Steno keycaps (OR 1u standard keycaps)
- 1 3D printed enclosure (Optional, 3 parts)  
~210g of filament total for 3D printed parts
